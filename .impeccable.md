# Хальмг Тиль Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are Kalmykia students and young adults who often know little or no Kalmyk, may feel awkward about that, and need a low-pressure way to start with living words and phrases. Secondary users are adults who want to reconnect with the language, and project stakeholders who need to see a serious cultural-education product rather than a toy clone of mainstream language apps.

### Brand Personality

Warm, rooted, calm. The interface should feel like an everyday entry point into the language: respectful, approachable, and culturally specific without becoming museum-like or souvenir-heavy.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use Kalmykia's visual identity as a system, not decoration: golden yellow, sky blue, white lotus, restrained red accents, steppe light, geometric ornament, and references to traditional forms. Avoid generic pastel edtech, Duolingo-like cartoon pressure, glassmorphism-heavy layouts, and random ornament pasted onto every card.

### Design Principles

1. Lower the entry barrier: the app must normalize "I almost do not know Kalmyk" as a valid starting point.
2. Teach through small daily wins: one phrase, a few words, one repeat session.
3. Make the dictionary useful but not intimidating: search, examples, favourites, and pronunciation support matter more than raw database scale.
4. Treat culture as context: symbols and facts should explain why a word matters, not just decorate the screen.
5. Keep the product credible: it should be good enough to show to schools, grant stakeholders, and local cultural partners.
